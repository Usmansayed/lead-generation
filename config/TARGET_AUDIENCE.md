# Target audience: who we’re looking for

## What “business” means here

**Business = any type**, not just restaurants, cafes, or “local business.” We want:

- **Professional services**: law firms, architecture firms, consulting firms, accounting practices, marketing agencies
- **Small business**: retail, service, trades, construction, e‑commerce, Etsy/shopify sellers
- **Any firm or practice** that’s opening, launching, or growing and might need a website or developer

We **exclude**:

- **Very technical businesses** (e.g. dev shops, SaaS product companies, “we build software” as core product)
- **Large MNCs** (multinational corporations) — we focus on small to mid-size businesses and firms

## The problem with tech/CS communities

When we fetched from **tech and indie communities** (e.g. r/SideProject, r/indiehackers, r/IMadeThis), most posts were from **developers or technical founders** (“I built this app…”, “Just shipped my MVP…”). Those people are **builders**, not our ideal leads. Our ideal leads are **non-technical business owners** — anyone running a business (law, architecture, consulting, shop, service, etc.) who might need a website or custom software.

## Who we target (all business types)

| We want (leads) | We avoid |
|-----------------|----------|
| New business / grand opening / “we just opened” (any type) | “I built this app” / “I shipped my side project” |
| Law firm, architecture firm, consulting firm, new practice | Developers, indie hackers, CS students |
| Small business (retail, service, professional, e‑commerce) | Very technical businesses, large MNCs |
| “Need a developer” / “looking for agency” in business context | Open source, side projects, weekend hacks |

## All 5 platforms (equal focus)

We focus on **all five platforms** equally: **LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram, Facebook, Reddit.** Each has:

- **LinkedIn**: “New firm”, “opened our practice”, “launching our firm”, “proud to announce”, “new venture”, “new chapter”
- **Twitter**: #smallbusiness, #grandopening, #nowopen, #lawfirm, #consulting, #architecture, plus phrase search for new firm / opened practice
- **Instagram**: grand opening, new business, small business, new firm, open for business (hashtags + phrases)
- **Facebook**: Grand opening, new business, open for business, new firm, opened our practice, new practice, come visit us
- **Reddit**: Subreddits for small business, professional services (LawFirm, consulting, Accounting, Construction, marketing), sellers (Etsy, shopify, ecommerce), regional business — plus keywords for new firm, new practice, opened our office

So we’re not “Reddit-only” — we use the same broad “any business type” focus across **LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram, Facebook, and Reddit**.

## Summary

- **Before**: Lots of “I built this” from tech/CS communities → poor fit.
- **After**: **Any business type** (law, architecture, firm, small business) on **all 5 platforms**; exclude very technical businesses and large MNCs. Same idea everywhere: people who run a business (of any kind) and might need a website or developer.

Controlled in **`search_strategy.yaml`** (themes, platform_expansions, Reddit groups) and **`phase1_sources.yaml`** (default keywords per platform). See **SEARCH_STRATEGY.md** for tuning.
